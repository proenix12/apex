Calculating Your Apex Pack Journey: An Estimation

To understand your proximity to potentially receiving Heirloom Shards, particularly concerning the 500-pack guarantee, it's crucial to estimate the total number of regular Apex Packs you've opened. This estimation considers packs earned through player leveling and direct purchases.

A. Packs Earned Through Player Level Progression (for a player at Level 220+)

Apex Legends rewards players with free Apex Packs as they increase their player level. The system provides a total of 199 Apex Packs by reaching level 500. The distribution is tiered:

  • Tier 1 (Levels 2-20): One Apex Pack is awarded for every level gained. This tier yields a total of 19 packs (Level 2 to Level 20 inclusive).
  • Tier 2 (Levels 22-300): One Apex Pack is awarded for every two levels gained (e.g., at level 22, 24, 26, and so on).

Calculation for Level 220:

If a player is exactly at Level 220:

  • Packs from Levels 2-20: 19 packs.
  • Packs from Levels 22-220: To calculate this, we find how many pack-awarding levels there are between 22 and 220 (inclusive). This is ((220 - 22) / 2) + 1 = (198 / 2) + 1 = 99 + 1 = 100 packs.
  • Total from leveling to 220: 19 + 100 = 119 packs.

Consideration for "Level 220+":

If "220+" means slightly above level 220 (e.g., up to level 229), the number of packs from leveling would be:

  • Level 221: Still 119 packs.
  • Level 222: 120 packs (+1 from level 220).
  • Level 228: 123 packs (+4 from level 220).

For our scenario, we'll use 119 packs as a baseline from leveling, assuming the player is around level 220-221.

B. Packs from Direct Purchases

The player scenario states 100 Apex Packs were purchased directly.

C. Other Regular Apex Packs (Battle Pass, Events, etc.)

This calculation does not include packs that might have been earned from Battle Passes, Treasure Packs, special events, or other promotions, unless these specifically awarded standard Apex Packs. If the player has earned additional standard Apex Packs through these means, those should be added to the total. For this analysis, we'll focus on the explicitly stated level and purchased packs.

D. Total Estimated Packs for the Scenario (Level 220 + 100 Purchased)

Based on the information:

Packs from Leveling (approx. Lvl 220): 119 packs

Packs from Purchases: 100 packs

Estimated Total Regular Apex Packs Opened: 119 + 100 = 219 packs.

If the player is slightly higher, say level 228, this could be 123 + 100 = 223 packs.

So, the player has likely opened around 219 to 223 regular Apex Packs.

Understanding the Heirloom Shard System

Knowing your pack count is one part; understanding how Heirloom Shards are awarded is the other.

A. The Random Drop Rate: 0.045%

Officially, Electronic Arts states that the random chance of receiving 150 Heirloom Shards from a regular Apex Pack is 0.045%. This translates to approximately 1 in 2,222 packs. Each pack opening (that doesn't trigger the pity system) is an independent event with this very low probability.

B. The 500-Pack Pity System

To ensure all players eventually receive Heirloom Shards, Apex Legends has a "pity system." This guarantees that a player will receive 150 Heirloom Shards after opening their 500th regular Apex Pack if they haven't received them randomly before that point.

  • If Heirloom Shards are obtained from a pack before the 500th pack, the pity counter resets to zero.
  • The count is per account and tracks all eligible regular Apex Packs opened since the account's creation or last Heirloom Shard acquisition from a pack.

C. Impact of Collection Event Heirlooms on Pity Counter

Apex Legends often features Collection Events where players can obtain a specific new Heirloom by collecting all (usually 24) event-exclusive cosmetic items.

Important: Acquiring an Heirloom directly through a Collection Event does NOT reset your 500-pack pity counter for Heirloom Shards from regular Apex Packs. These are considered separate acquisition methods.

Probability Analysis for the Player's Scenario (Estimated 219 Packs)

Let's analyze the player's situation, assuming they've opened approximately 219 packs.

A. Chance of Not Receiving Shards Randomly Yet

The probability of *not* getting Heirloom Shards in a single pack is 1 - 0.00045 = 0.99955 (or 99.955%). The cumulative probability of *not* receiving shards after 219 independent trials (packs) can be calculated as:

(0.99955)219 ≈ 0.9063

This means there is approximately a 90.63% chance that the player would NOT have received Heirloom Shards randomly after opening 219 packs. Conversely, the chance of having received them randomly by this point is about 1 - 0.9063 = 0.0937 or 9.37%.

This relatively low chance of a random drop by 219 packs explains why many players in this range haven't seen shards yet.

B. Progress Towards the 500-Pack Pity System

With an estimated 219 packs opened:

Packs remaining to reach the 500-pack guarantee = 500 - 219 = 281 packs.

The player is significantly less than halfway to the guaranteed pity drop.

Conclusion: Why No Heirloom Shards Yet?

Based on the calculations and the mechanics of the Heirloom Shard system, the primary reasons why a player at level 220+ with 100 purchased packs (totaling ~219 packs) likely hasn't received Heirloom Shards are:

  1. Statistical Rarity: The random drop rate of 0.045% is extremely low. It is statistically probable (around 90.63% chance) to open 219 packs and not receive a random Heirloom Shard drop.
  2. Distance from Pity Cap: The player is still approximately 281 packs away from the 500-pack guarantee. Most players will likely receive their first Heirloom Shards through this pity system rather than a lucky random drop.

While it can be frustrating, not having Heirloom Shards at this stage is a common experience and aligns with the designed probabilities and pity system of Apex Legends.
